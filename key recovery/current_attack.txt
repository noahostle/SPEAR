Current SEPAR Attack Summary
============================

1. Practical K8 Recovery
------------------------
Target:
    K8 = key[14] || key[15]

Threat model:
    Chosen-IV, chosen-plaintext access to the real cipher as implemented in SEPAR/main.c.

Core idea:
    Use independent one-block oracle queries under a fixed small IV family.
    For each IV surface, build a first-block ciphertext codebook over a chosen plaintext set.
    Exhaustively score K8 candidates with the stage-8 differential peel and aggregate across surfaces.

Files:
    build_firstblock_dataset.py
        Builds correct first-block datasets from the DLL/oracle.
        Relative dataset outputs go under key recovery/data.

    k8_row_search.c / k8_row_search.exe
        Multicore exhaustive K8 search over one or more first-block datasets.

Recommended fixed IV family:
    iv[1] = 0x0001
    iv[5] = 0x1000

Recommended dataset size:
    sample size 768 or 1024 per IV surface

Online cost:
    1552 one-block queries for sample size 768
    2064 one-block queries for sample size 1024

Offline cost on this machine:
    About 30.5 hours for the 2-surface, sample-size-768 setting
    About 39.6 hours for the 2-surface, sample-size-1024 setting

Status:
    This is the current practical front-end for exact K8 recovery.
    It is operationally key-agnostic: the same IV family and search procedure are reused across tested keys.


2. Exact Recursive K6..K1 Recovery
----------------------------------
Target:
    Recover K6, K5, K4, K3, K2, K1 once K8 and K7 are known.

Threat model:
    Chosen-IV, chosen-plaintext access to the real cipher as implemented in SEPAR/main.c.
    K8 and K7 are assumed already recovered.

Core idea:
    Use two full-domain block-2 codebooks for prefixes 0x0000 and 0x0001.
    Peel one stage at a time and recursively align the two codebooks with an exact delta search.
    After alignment at each stage:
    - scan the common stage shift
    - scan the row maximum for the true left 16-bit key word
    - scan the column maximum for the true right 16-bit key word

    This exact recursive bridge works for:
        K6 -> K5 -> K4 -> K3 -> K2

    The final K1 stage is recovered with an exact one-stage SMT solve on the final aligned stage-1 codebook.

Files:
    outer_half_recursive_break.py
        End-to-end seeded recursive attack driver.
        Generates intermediate .bin codebooks under key recovery/data/outer_half_recursive_break by default.

    v45_delta_search.c / v45_delta_search.exe
        Exact 16-bit delta search between two aligned codebooks.

    multi_dataset_peel_tool.c / multi_dataset_peel_tool_omp.exe
        Shift scan and row/column scans for the current stage.

    stage_exact_solver.py
        Exact one-stage solver used for the final K1 recovery.

Online cost:
    131072 two-block oracle queries
    This is exactly:
        2 prefixes * 65536 suffixes

Observed validated recovery on the built-in key:
    K6 = F44A233E
    K5 = 50C512DB
    K4 = E95307FD
    K3 = 02DD3972
    K2 = DA5D96D7
    K1 = E8B9B733

Observed runtime on this machine:
    About 980 seconds total for the seeded recursion from K7 down through K1
    The final K1 SMT solve took about 121 seconds

Status:
    This recursive attack is exact and validated against the real DLL once K8 and K7 are known.
    The remaining unsolved front-end is exact generic K7 recovery from K8-known access.
